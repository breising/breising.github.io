Okay, I know what your thinking - WTheck? Are they kidding? And you're right to think that. The fact is that MOST clear aligner treatments require putting these "buttons" on the front side of at least some of your teeth! And these things are even MORE visible than the clear trays.

So why are these attachment things needed? The answer is that it's a simple issue of needing mechanical leverage to move teeth. The clear plastic tray must be able to grab hold of each tooth and apply pressure (force) in the correct directions to push (or pull) it to its new, corrected position.

Consider the game of bobing for apples. They are really slippery when wet and you just can't get your mouth around'em - no leverage. It's a similar problem with clear thermoplastics and teeth. Teeth have a conical shape (tapered side walls) that cannot be grasped well, if at all, by a clear aligner.

No leverage. The clear aligner (gray) slips off and the tooth (black) remains stationary.

Now imagine the bobbing for apples that are skewered with chopsticks. Now it's easy - just bite the stick and you've got full controll over the apple too. That's kind of how the attachments work. It's just a handle for the clear plastic to grasp hold and gain control over movement of the teeth.

Let's go a little deeper now on the topic of effectiveness. We've already learned that leverage is a huge problem for clear aligners. Now, let's discuss the issue of the pressure or force needed to move teeth.

So, the way clear aligners work is, first, your teeth are aligned on the computer. Then, the computer is able to create all the steps in between the original start position and the new aligned position. It moves the teeth by a fraction of a millimeter in each step until it finally aligns all the teeth. Then for each 'step' they make an aligner. Pretty simple. No magic. Each aligner will then create pressure, because it is designed to be slightly mal-fitting. This pressure moves your teeth to the next step where a new aligner can take over.

Now then, why make all these step? Why not do it all in one single step? Well, as you might know, there's a very serious limitation here. You see, the plastic can't bend very far. And even the small amount that it does bend creates a LOT OF PRESSURE - which means it hurts. And if it bends too far, it will break. So you can see why they need all these steps. Each step creates only a very small amount of movement using a big painful force. And this process of pulsating pain must be repeated again and again - every two weeks. Put in new tray, teeth hurt bad for several days, pressure dissipates over then next ten days, ...repeat. Sounds like fun eh?

That's a lot of steps.

The wire connects all the braces and creates the pressure that moves the teeth. Shape memory alloy wires have this unique ability to be bent into wildly contorted shapes and then bounce back to their original form.
